Child Mortality
Child mortality is an everyday tragedy of enormous scale that rarely makes the headlines. On average, there are 5.4 million children died before their fifth birthday. Therefore, the following visualisations are used to raise the public awareness on the seriousness of this issue.
Child Mortality Rate Around the World, 2016
Relationship Between Economic and Child Mortality

A strong negative correlation between child mortality and GDP is displayed below. This shows that those richer countries located in Europe tend to be having lower child mortality rate as compared to poorer countries where majority of them are located in Africa.

Child Mortality Statistics by Country

Most countries in Africa tend to have the highest child mortality rate as compared to other continents. On the other hand, we can see that some Asian countries are performing as good as the European countries in terms of having low child mortality rate.

Ranking of Child Mortality by Country, 2000 - 2016

The performance of different countries in terms of child mortality rate from the last 15 years is shown below. It can be clearly seen that there is more fluctuation among the African countries in the ranking throughout the years.

Child Deaths in Sierra Leone, 2000 - 2016

The yearly difference in the number of child deaths in Sierra Leone is shown below. We can see that there is a continuous decrease from 2011 onwards followed by a major increment due to ebola epidemic that happened in 2014.

Overall Child Mortality by Life Stage

In less than two decades, child mortality has reduced by almost halved. Besides, it can be seen that Africa and Europe each having the highest and lowest proportion of child mortality respectively in three different life stages.
